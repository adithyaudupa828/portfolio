Level Design

1. AAA TITLE, Mars Games

Roles: Level design, block out, prop placement, QA/testing.

As a level designer for an unannounced title at Mars Games, I designed 5 unique POIs (points of interest) for different types of environments.

Levels: Stockyard & Stables, Blacksmith, Copper & Tin Mines, Farm, and Swamp Forest.

1. Stockyard and Stables: A central hub where players can buy and upgrade horses, and buy essential supplies.
2. Blacksmith: A small blacksmith shop with weapons and other parts.
3. Copper and Tin Mines: A maze-like tunnel system with heavy combat and resources deep in the center.
4. Farm: A calm area without combat where players can interact and exchange items.
5. Swamp Forest: (Current POI) A combat focused area with poison clouds and creatures.

Tools: UE5, Blender, splines, modular kits, landscape tools, foliage tools.

Resources: Article by Amin Montazeri on level design process and principles. Video by Ryan Laley for tunnels.

Images: Stockyard & Stables and Blacksmith.

Images: Copper & Tin Mine, Farm, and Swamp Forest.

Post-Mortem / What I learnt:

Working in a large-scale studio of over 150 people taught me how a professional AAA pipeline functions and how my contributions fit into the larger picture. I was directly mentored in industry-standard level design techniques, from initial planning and research to creating detailed in-engine block outs. With a large team of over 20 level designers and environment artists, I learned that constant communication is absolutely essential to ensure design intent isn't lost. This experience solidified the importance of thorough documentation, as it's the primary tool for aligning multiple departments toward a single, cohesive vision.

2. DAREDEVIL, Solo Fan Game

Roles: Level design, plan and layout, block out, environment art.

Levels: Home and Shipyard & Warehouses.

1. Home: Inspired by Ready Or Not's headquarters for loadout, mission select, and interactions, this serves as Daredevil’s base of operations, an interactive hub with distinct zones that each serve a purpose.
2. Shipyard and Warehouses: Set in New York’s docks, this is a hostile combat environment filled with enemies and narrative clues. The docked ship contains a challenging mini-boss fight. Smaller warehouses hide civilians in need of rescue, as well as valuable hints tied to the larger story.

Tools: UE5, Blender, Fab, splines, landscape tools, modeling tools.

Images: Home Level.

Images: Shipyard & Warehouse Level (Current Level).

Post-Mortem / What I learnt:

I learned how heavily level design must support core mechanics, as I had to redesign spaces multiple times to properly accommodate the unique parkour and grappling hook systems. I learned to wear multiple hats, handling everything from the initial level block out to the final environment art, lighting, and placement of actors. The process taught me the value of rapid, self-driven iteration, as I had to constantly playtest my own work to find and fix flaws in player flow and pacing. This project was a critical lesson in personal scope management, forcing me to balance my ambitious vision for a fan game with the practical realities of a solo developer's limited time.

3. ELIXIR BLITZ: SAYA'S TRIALS, Group Project

Roles: Level design, environment art, prop placement, general QA/testing.

A mystical forest where players play as a fairy on a quest to gather many rare glowing ingredients. The environment shifts between serene lakes, cascading waterfalls, and shadowy forests, creating both beauty and mystery. Once the ingredients are collected, players must reach the portal to complete the run, crafting their finds into potions.

Tools: UE5, Blender, splines, landscape tools, modeling tools, Fab, blueprints.

Images: Mystical Forest.

Post-Mortem / What I learnt:

Developing a complete game in just one month was an intense but invaluable exercise in time management and rapid, decisive implementation. Working in a small, agile team of seven meant my level design contributions had a direct and immediate impact, requiring a high degree of personal accountability. I learned to communicate efficiently with programmers and artists, making smart compromises to ensure our level's features were achievable within the tight schedule. This project taught me how to prioritize the core player experience and focus on shipping a complete, functional level rather than getting lost in non-essential details.
